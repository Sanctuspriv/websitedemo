Frequently Asked Questions

Who can take the morning after pill?

Our pharmacist will be able to offer you the morning after pill if you are above the age of 16. The morning after pill will not be suitable if:

  • You are already pregnant
  • You've had unprotected sex more than 120 hours (5 days) ago.
  • You have had an allergic reaction to any of the ingredients (the pharmacist can advise you on this)
  • You have already had the morning after pill during this menstrual cycle. (You may have to speak to your doctor or nearest family planning clinic.)

You must tell the pharmacist or your doctor if you fall into any one of the below catagories BEFORE you take the morning after pill.

  • You have previously had an ectopic pregnancy
  • You live with a digestive disorder, for example, Crohn's disease
  • If you are taking any other medications including but not limited to arbiturates, Primidone, Phenytoin, Carbemazepine/Tegretol, Ciclosporin, Griseofulvin, herbal remedies containing St John's Wort, Rifampicin or Rifabutinand Ritonavir.
  • If you're breastfeeding
  • If you're unsure if the morning after pill is suitable for you.
Is the morning after pill effective?

The morning after pill is not 100% effective, but there is a greater chance of preventing a pregnancy if it is taken within 12 hours of unprotected sex.

A trial undertaken by the World Health Organization (WHO) indicated that levonorgestrel (the ingredient in Levonelle) prevented:

95% of expected pregnancies when taken within 24 hours of sex

85% if taken within 25-48 hours

58% if taken within 49-72 hours

ellaOne® if used correctly, is very likely that it will be effective. However, it is not effective in every case: of 100 women receiving ellaOne® up to 5 days after unprotected sex, approximately 2 will become pregnant. So, as long as you use ellaOne® correctly, it is very likely that it will be effective.

The morning after pill will not stop a pregnancy if you are already pregnant.

How do I take the morning after pill?

Always read the patient information leaflet that comes with the tablet.

Take the tablet as soon as possible after unprotected sex, ideally within 12 hours and before 72 hours has passed. Ensure you swallow the tablet whole with water. If you are sick within 3 hours of taking the tablet, you will need to take another one. Consult the pharmacist or your doctor for more advice and to obtain another tablet.

How does the morning after pill work?

Morning after pills work majoritively by stopping or delaying the body from releasing an egg (Ovulation). Levenorgestrel may also affect the lining of the womb, preventing a fertilised egg from implanting itself and growing.

What are the side effects?

Not everybody will experience side effects, although some may experience the following:

  • Dizziness and fatigue
  • Feeling or being sick
  • Lower stomach pain
  • Diarrhoea
  • Headaches
  • Unexpected bleeding (not related to your monthly cycle)
  • Tender breasts

If you experience any difficulty in breathing, swelling of the face, neck, tongue or throat, seek immediate medical attention.

The morning after pill may disturb your normal period cycle, meaning you may begin sooner or later than expected. If your next period is more than five days late, or is unusually light or heavy, or if you suspect you may be pregnant, you should take a pregnancy test and speak to your doctor as soon as possible.
