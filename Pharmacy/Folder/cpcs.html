CPCS Via NHS 111

Pharmacists are highly skilled medical professionals, who can help with a wide range of issues. Our pharmacists can help treat minor illness, or give you an emergency supply of medication on the day, without the need to see a GP. You can discuss any concerns about your health with the pharmacist from the privacy of our consultation room. We provide this service through NHS 111 referrals, meaning you should call NHS 111 first to assess the issue, where you will then be referred to our pharmacy.

What can our pharmacist help with?

This list is by no means exhaustive, and if you're unsure we suggest going over your condition with the NHS 111 operator first. Our pharmacists can help with minor ailments and illnesses such as:

  • Bites and stings
  • Colds and flu like illness
  • Congestion (Blocked or runny noses, hayfever or a build up of mucus)
  • Excessive ear wax
  • Eye issues such as conjunctivitis, dry, teary or sore eyes etc
  • Gastric and bowl issues (constipation, indigestion, vomiting, haemorrhoids and more)
  • Gynae and thrush conditions
  • General muscular pain like sprains and muscle strain
  • Skin conditions like acne, athletes foot, rashes and verrucas
  • Mouth and throat conditions such as cold sores, ulcers or sore throats

This will include:

  • Children under 1 year of age
  • Immunosuppressed patients
  • Severe symptoms
  • Pregnant patients
  • Symptoms and conditions that have lasted longer than 3 weeks
  • illness that has not responded to over-the-counter medication.
