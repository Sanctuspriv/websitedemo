Discharge Medication Service

What is the DMS?

The discharge medication service is a free NHS service put in place to help you adjust after being discharged from hospital. If you find yourself unsure about your medications after being discharged from hospital, we're happy to help with that.

The Discharge Medication Service is completely free, and exists as a way to help you understand your new treatment, reduce the possibility of readmission to hospital and improve communication when a patients medicines have been changed following hospital admission.

What can we do?

We will check your current medications against the new medicine(s) you have been perscribed to ensure there's no harmful interactions, and where necessary, we will contact your medical practice to ensure your safety.

If you wish, we can dispose of old medications you will no longer be using after being discharged from hospital, to make sure there's no confusion with which medications you are or are not taking.

If you are elligible, we will offer the New Medicinies Service (NMS) to you. This is so we can help you take your medication as easily as possible, or fit your new treatment around your lifestyle. For example, if you find yourself in need of an inhaler after being discharged, we can show you how to use that inhaler, and give you any advice or answer any questions you may have. You can read more about the NMS here.
